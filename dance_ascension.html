PHASE 1 · Months 1–2
Body Awakening
Destroy stiffness. Build isolations, flexibility, rhythm. No choreo yet — pure body training.
PHASE 2 · Months 3–5
Foundation Styles
Hip-Hop, Afrobeats, K-Pop girl group simultaneously. Groove, body rhythm, sharp precision.
PHASE 3 · Months 6–8
Choreo Beast
Full songs start to finish. African dance added. Boy group styles. Expression and stage presence begins.
PHASE 4 · Months 9–13
Style Expansion
Dancehall, Waacking, Sensual/Slow dance, Jazz-Funk, Contemporary, Heels. Your signature emerges.
PHASE 5 · Months 14–18
Stage Command
Own the stage. No weak side. No blank face. High energy AND slow dance — both mastered.
PHASE 6 · Months 19–20
Stage Ready
20+ songs, original choreo, any style on command. You are the professional now.

Step 2 — Film Yourself First
What to record and how to set up
Camera Setup
• Full body visible head to toe, 1ft above head
• Hip height or slightly above — never floor level
• Plain wall behind you for best visibility
• Natural window light from the FRONT
What to Record
• Full run-through of what you're assessing — no stopping
• Watch it back at 0.5x speed first, then normal speed
• Use CapCut (free) to slow down and see details
• Compare side-by-side with your reference tutorial
